Who am I?

Hello! My name is Kyra Lee and I am a Technical Writer in Toronto. When I was a kid, my dream was to speak 100 languages so I could communicate with everyone. Now, that's exactly what I do at work. Well... It's close enough.

My education at Seneca College led me to working part-time at a content migration company as a technical writer. My main responsibilities include creating technical training materials, software demo videos, and user documentation for clients in the software, hardware, and aerospace industries. I also assist in project management by developing and monitoring project plans to ensure they are completed on time and within budget.

I attended Queen's Univerity for my undergrad and received a degree in Language, Literature, and Cultural Studies, with a minor in Global Development Studies. Before discovering Technical Communication, I did internships in multiple fields, including marketing, public relations, intercultural communications, and law. This exposed me to various research and industrious writing styles and allowed me to grow as a writer.

In my free time, I like to research and develop new skills. I believe that learning is perpetual and am always trying to keep myself busy! I still love learning languages, and I currently speak 5 (I know... still a long way to go to achieve my childhood dream). When I'm not working or studying, you could find me dancing or cuddling with my cats.
